# Scoring Methods

Scoring methods determine how the solver optimizes your factory design.

## What is a Scoring Method?

When your factory has multiple valid solutions that meet your goals, the solver uses a scoring method to choose the "best" one. Different scoring methods prioritize different objectives.

## Available Scoring Methods

### Lowest Use of Inputs
Minimizes the total consumption of input resources. Best when you want to:
- Reduce raw material usage
- Minimize operating costs
- Create the most resource-efficient factory

### Infrastructure
Minimizes the total infrastructure requirements. Optimizes for:
- Lowest electricity consumption
- Fewest workers needed
- Minimal maintenance requirements
- Reduced computing needs

Best when infrastructure is your limiting factor.

### Footprint
Minimizes the physical space used by buildings. Useful for:
- Compact factory designs
- Limited building space
- Minimizing construction costs

### Outputs
Maximizes the total production of all outputs. Use when you want:
- Maximum production capacity
- Highest surplus of byproducts
- Most productive factory possible

## Choosing a Scoring Method

The right scoring method depends on your situation:
- **Early game**: Use "Lowest Use of Inputs" to conserve resources
- **Mid game**: Use "Infrastructure" when power or workers are limited
- **Late game**: Use "Outputs" to maximize production
- **Space-constrained**: Use "Footprint" on cramped islands

## How Scoring Works

The solver assigns a numeric score to each potential solution, then finds the solution with the lowest score (for minimization methods) or highest score (for maximization methods).
