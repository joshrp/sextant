# Space Research

Rockets, launches, the station and crew all work like any other recipe here — giving a per minute rate. This makes the numbers a little different from in-game, but easier to plan throughput with. 

## Launches are averaged

In game, a launch is a single event: build a rocket, load it, fire it. Here, a launch is a **rate**. If a T1 rocket carries 40 parts and the station only needs 4/min, the planner shows `0.1 launches/min` — one launch every ten minutes on average.

That's fine. The numbers add up over time. Rockets, fuel and cargo all scale the same way — a tenth of a launch uses a tenth of a rocket and a tenth of the fuel.

## Crew is part of the station

The crew working on the station is **station infrastructure** — sourced from your settlements like the workers in any other building. The station's worker count grows with its level, and shows up on the station node like Workers on any other machine.

You still need to launch crew, though: in game, a fresh crew rotation goes up roughly **once every 24 months** regardless of station size. That's set as a floor here — even at low levels the planner runs at least 1/24 of a crew launch per minute, so the rocket and fuel cost shows up in the bill.

Pick the tier (T1 or T2) you want for crew launches based on fuel cost — it doesn't change the cadence.

## Research points come in big steps

The station is one building. Its **level** sets how much it produces — you choose it with the `−` `+` buttons on the node.

- **L1 and L2 produce nothing.** They're only there to show the cost of getting to L3.
- **L3 gives 48 RP/min.** Each level up adds another 48 (L4 = 96, L5 = 144, and so on).

There is no in-between. If you want 50 RP/min you have to build for 96 and pay for the rest. For small goals, that overhead is most of the bill — it's usually worth raising the goal to the next step before deciding it's too much.

## Tips

- Only see the T1 launch in the picker? Click the `2 ⌄` chevron next to the machine icon to show T2 as well.
- Empty input handles on the station are normal — they stay so edges don't move when you change level.
